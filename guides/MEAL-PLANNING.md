## Complete Meal Combinations

### Combination 1: Simple Weeknight

**For:** 1-2 people, 20 minutes total

```
Main: 番茄鸡蛋面 (tomato egg noodles)
Side: Quick pickled cucumber

Total prep: 20 minutes
```

#### Quick Pickled Cucumber Recipe
```
1 cucumber, smashed and cut
2 cloves garlic, smashed
1 tbsp soy sauce
1 tbsp rice vinegar
1/2 tsp sesame oil
1/2 tsp sugar
Pinch of chili flakes

Mix all, let sit while cooking noodles.
```

---

### Combination 2: Hearty Dinner

**For:** 2-4 people, 30 minutes total

```
Main: 番茄鸡蛋面
Protein: Pan-fried dumplings (frozen, 10 min)
Vegetable: Stir-fried bok choy with garlic

Total prep: 30 minutes
```

#### Stir-Fried Bok Choy
```
2 heads baby bok choy, halved
2 cloves garlic, sliced
1 tbsp oil
1 tbsp oyster sauce
Pinch of salt

High heat, 3 minutes total.
```

---

### Combination 3: Family Style

**For:** 4-6 people, 45 minutes total

```
Main: Large pot of 番茄鸡蛋面
Meat: Red-braised pork belly (or bought)
Vegetable: Dry-fried green beans
Cold: Smashed cucumber salad
Extra: Steamed rice (for variety)

Total prep: 45 minutes (with shortcuts)
```

### Combination 4: Vegetarian Feast

## Meal Planning by Time

### 15-Minute Meal
```
Just the noodles:
- Use instant noodles
- Canned tomatoes
- Already-beaten eggs
- Pre-minced garlic

Serve with: Nothing needed
```

### 30-Minute Meal
```
Noodles + 1 side:
- Make noodles (15 min)
- While simmering: quick cucumber salad
- Or: stir-fry frozen dumplings

Serve with: Tea
```

### 45-Minute Meal
```
Noodles + 2 sides:
- Start: prep all vegetables
- Cook: protein first (dumplings or quick stir-fry)
- Then: noodles
- Last: quick vegetable stir-fry

Serve with: Tea, pickles
```

### Weekend Feast (1 hour+)
```
Full family meal:
- Cold dishes made ahead
- Red-braised meat (while prepping rest)
- Noodles made fresh
- Stir-fried greens last
- Rice if needed

Serve with: Multiple dishes, communal style
```

## Weekly Meal Prep Ideas

### Sunday Prep Session

**Make Ahead:**
```
□ Pickle vegetables (lasts 1 week)
□ Cook large batch of eggs (for quick egg drop)
□ Blanch and freeze tomatoes (out of season)
□ Make stock and freeze in portions
□ Pre-mince garlic and ginger
```

## Seasonal Considerations

### Spring (春季)
```
Light and fresh:
- Use fresh spring tomatoes (when available)
- Light vegetable sides
- Pair with: steamed asparagus, pea shoots
```

### Summer (夏季)
```
Keep it cool:
- Consider cold noodle variation
- More cold side dishes
- Lighter soup, less oil
- Pair with: cold cucumber, iced tea
```

### Fall (秋季)
```
Heartier meals:
- Richer stock
- Add mushrooms
- Warming spices
- Pair with: roasted squash, warm sides
```

### Winter (冬季)
```
Maximum comfort:
- Use canned tomatoes (fresh unavailable)
- Extra stock, bigger portions
- Add meat if desired
- Pair with: braised dishes, hot tea, steamed buns
```

## Special Occasion Planning

```
Extra soothing version:
- Light broth, easy on stomach
- Soft, well-cooked noodles
- Extra ginger for immunity
- Simple seasonings only

Serve with: Hot water with honey and lemon
```

```
Maximum comfort:
- Rich, thick version
- Extra egg
- Side of dumplings
```

```
- Proper table setting
```

## Leftover Management

### Can You Make Ahead?

| Component | Make Ahead? | How |
|-----------|-------------|-----|
| Tomato base | Yes (2-3 days) | Store separately |
| Noodles | No | Cook fresh |
| Eggs | No | Cook fresh |
| Stock | Yes (5 days) | Refrigerate |
| Assembled soup | No | Gets soggy |

### Leftover Soup (No Noodles)

```
If you have leftover broth without noodles:
- Refrigerate up to 3 days
- Reheat, cook fresh noodles
- Add fresh egg

Bonus use:
- Use as base for rice soup (泡饭)
- Add to stir-fry as liquid
- Poach eggs in it
```

## Make It a Tradition

### Weekly Tomato Noodle Night

Many families have a designated noodle night:

```
Benefits:
- Kids know what to expect
- Easy meal planning
- Can vary the style each week
- Builds cooking rhythm
- Creates family memories
```

### Teaching Kids to Cook

Progression:
1. **Age 5+**: Wash vegetables, crack eggs
2. **Age 8+**: Beat eggs, measure ingredients
3. **Age 10+**: Cook noodles (supervised)
4. **Age 12+**: Full recipe with guidance
5. **Age 14+**: Independent cooking
