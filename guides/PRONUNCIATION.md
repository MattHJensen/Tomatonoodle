## The Main Dish

### 番茄鸡蛋面 (Standard Mandarin)

| Character | Pinyin | Tone | Sounds Like |
|-----------|--------|------|-------------|
| 番 | fān | 1st (high flat) | "fahn" |
| 茄 | qié | 2nd (rising) | "chyeh" |
| 鸡 | jī | 1st (high flat) | "jee" |
| 蛋 | dàn | 4th (falling) | "dahn" |
| 面 | miàn | 4th (falling) | "myen" |

**Full pronunciation:** fān-chyeh jee-dahn myen

**Audio approximation:** "fahn-CHYEH jee-DAHN myen"

---

| Character | Pinyin | Tone | Sounds Like |
|-----------|--------|------|-------------|
| 西 | xī | 1st | "shee" |
| 红 | hóng | 2nd | "hohng" |
| 柿 | shì | 4th | "shih" |
| 鸡 | jī | 1st | "jee" |
| 蛋 | dàn | 4th | "dahn" |
| 面 | miàn | 4th | "myen" |

**Full pronunciation:** shee-hohng-shih jee-dahn myen

**Note:** 西红柿 literally means "western red persimmon" - an older name for tomato reflecting its foreign origin.

## Cooking Terms

### 炒出沙 (Stir-fry Until Sandy)

| Character | Pinyin | Meaning |
|-----------|--------|---------|
| 炒 | chǎo | stir-fry |
| 出 | chū | to come out |
| 沙 | shā | sand |

**Pronunciation:** chow choo shah

**Meaning:** To cook tomatoes until they develop a sandy, grainy texture

## Tone Guide for Mandarin

| Tone | Mark | Sound | Example |
|------|------|-------|---------|
| 1st | ā | High, flat | mā (mother) |
| 2nd | á | Rising | má (hemp) |
| 3rd | ǎ | Dip then rise | mǎ (horse) |
| 4th | à | Falling | mà (scold) |
| Neutral | a | Light, quick | ma (question particle) |

**Practice with 面 (noodles):**
- It's 4th tone (miàn)
- Start high, drop sharply
- Like saying "myen!" emphatically

## Common Mispronunciations to Avoid

| Word | Wrong | Right | Tip |
|------|-------|-------|-----|
| 番茄 | "fan chee" | "fahn chyeh" | The 茄 has a "yeh" sound |
| 鸡蛋 | "jee dan" | "jee dahn" | Stretch the "ah" sound |
| 面 | "mee-an" | "myen" | One syllable, not two |
| 炒 | "chow" (like cow) | "chow" (rhymes with "how") | More "ah" in the vowel |
| 蒜 | "swan" | "swahn" | More open "ah" sound |

## Regional Variations

### Northern China
- Heavier, more emphatic tones
- 儿化音 (er-hua): Adding "r" sound to words
- 面 might sound like "myer"

### Southern China
- Softer consonants
- Less distinction between some sounds
- May substitute 'n' for 'ng' endings

### Taiwan
- Softer overall
- Different vocabulary choices
- 西红柿 less common than 番茄

### Overseas Chinese
- May mix with local accent
- Vocabulary might differ
- Code-switching between languages

## Practice Sentences

### Level 1 (Beginner)
```
我要番茄鸡蛋面。
Wǒ yào fān qié jī dàn miàn.
(I want tomato egg noodles.)
```

### Level 2 (Intermediate)
```
请把番茄炒出沙。
Qǐng bǎ fān qié chǎo chū shā.
(Please stir-fry the tomatoes until sandy.)
```

### Level 3 (Advanced)
```
我奶奶的番茄鸡蛋面是全家最爱。
Wǒ nǎi nai de fān qié jī dàn miàn shì quán jiā zuì ài.
(My grandmother's tomato egg noodles are the family's favorite.)
```
