## Detailed Noodle Profiles

### 1. 拉面 | Hand-Pulled Noodles (Lamian)

**Origin:** Lanzhou, Gansu Province
**Texture:** Chewy, springy, with slight bounce
**Width:** Variable (thin to thick)
**Color:** Off-white to pale yellow

#### Characteristics
```
Chewiness:    ████████░░ 8/10
Absorbency:   ██████░░░░ 6/10
Soup Hold:    ███████░░░ 7/10
Skill Level:  ██████████ 10/10
```

#### Best Pairings with Tomato Egg
- **Ideal for:** Clear broth tomato soups (Lanzhou style)
- **Cooking tip:** Pull to medium thickness for tomato soup; thin noodles get lost
- **Regional use:** Gansu, Qinghai, Xinjiang

#### How to Identify Quality
- Should stretch without breaking
- Smooth surface, no cracks
- Even thickness throughout
- Springs back when pressed

### 3. 龙须面 | Dragon Beard Noodles

**Origin:** Imperial Chinese cuisine
**Texture:** Extremely thin, delicate
**Width:** Hair-thin (< 1mm)
**Color:** White to pale yellow

#### Characteristics
```
Chewiness:    ███░░░░░░░ 3/10
Absorbency:   █████████░ 9/10
Soup Hold:    █████░░░░░ 5/10
Skill Level:  ███░░░░░░░ 3/10 (to cook)
```

#### Best Pairings with Tomato Egg
- **Ideal for:** Light, delicate tomato soups
- **Cooking tip:** Add to soup at the last moment; overcooks in seconds
- **Regional use:** Pan-Chinese, especially Shanghai

#### Why "Dragon Beard"
Named for their resemblance to the fine whiskers of a Chinese dragon. Originally hand-pulled through 12+ folding and stretching cycles.

### 5. 碱水面 | Fresh Alkaline Noodles

**Origin:** Southern China, Hong Kong
**Texture:** Bouncy, springy, slightly firm
**Width:** 2-3mm
**Color:** Yellow (from alkaline/kansui)

#### Characteristics
```
Chewiness:    ████████░░ 8/10
Absorbency:   █████░░░░░ 5/10
Soup Hold:    ███████░░░ 7/10
Skill Level:  ██░░░░░░░░ 2/10
```

#### Best Pairings with Tomato Egg
- **Ideal for:** Hong Kong-style tomato macaroni soup adaptation
- **Cooking tip:** Rinse after cooking to remove excess alkaline taste
- **Regional use:** Guangdong, Hong Kong, Macau

#### The Science
Kansui (枧水) - alkaline mineral water - creates the yellow color and distinctive texture. It's what makes wonton noodles bouncy.

## Noodle Pairing Matrix

### By Soup Style

| Soup Style | Best Noodles | Avoid |
|------------|--------------|-------|
| **Clear broth** | 拉面, 龙须面, 挂面 | 刀削面 (too heavy) |
| **Thick gravy (打卤)** | 刀削面, 宽面, 裤带面 | 龙须面 (too delicate) |
| **Egg drop soup** | 挂面, 碱水面, 伊面 | 面疙瘩 (redundant texture) |
| **Dry style (拌面)** | 宽面, 碱水面, 裤带面 | 龙须面, 伊面 |
| **Cold style** | 素面, 挂面 (chilled) | 面疙瘩, 刀削面 |

### By Cooking Skill

| Skill Level | Recommended Noodles |
|-------------|---------------------|
| **Beginner** | 挂面, 碱水面 (store-bought), 龙须面 |
| **Intermediate** | 刀削面 (store-bought), 宽面, 伊面 |
| **Advanced** | 拉面 (hand-pulled), 裤带面, 面疙瘩 |

### By Time Available

| Time | Best Choice | Notes |
|------|-------------|-------|
| **5 minutes** | 龙须面, instant noodles | Pre-boil water |
| **10 minutes** | 挂面, 碱水面 | Standard weeknight |
| **20 minutes** | 刀削面, 宽面 | Weekend cooking |
| **30+ minutes** | 拉面 (homemade), 面疙瘩 | Special occasions |

## Regional Noodle Traditions

### Northern China (华北)
- Preference for wheat noodles
- Thicker, chewier textures
- Hand-made traditions strong
- 刀削面, 拉面, 面疙瘩

### Northwestern China (西北)
- Lamb-friendly noodles
- Very chewy, substantial
- 裤带面, 拉面 (Lanzhou style)

### Eastern China (华东)
- Lighter, more delicate
- Absorb subtle flavors
- 龙须面, 阳春面

### Southern China (华南)
- Rice noodles more common
- Wheat noodles are thinner
- 河粉, 碱水面

### Sichuan/Southwest (西南)
- Balance of chewy and absorbent
- Must hold up to bold flavors
- 挂面, 碱水面, 小面

## Cooking Techniques

### The Perfect Boil

```
Water ratio:  1 liter per 100g noodles
Salt:         1 tsp per liter (optional but recommended)
Temperature:  Rolling boil
Stirring:     Immediately after adding, then every 30 seconds
```

### Timing Guide

| Noodle Type | Fresh | Dried |
|-------------|-------|-------|
| 龙须面 | 30-60 sec | 1-2 min |
| 挂面 | N/A | 3-4 min |
| 碱水面 | 2-3 min | 3-4 min |
| 刀削面 | 3-4 min | 4-5 min |
| 拉面 | 2-3 min | N/A |
| 面疙瘩 | 5-8 min | N/A |

### The Cold Water Rinse Debate

| Style | Rinse? | Why |
|-------|--------|-----|
| Soup noodles | No | Keeps starch for body |
| Dry/mixed noodles | Yes | Prevents sticking |
| Cold noodles | Yes | Stops cooking, firms texture |
| Northern style | Yes | Traditional preference |
| Southern style | No | Softer texture preferred |
