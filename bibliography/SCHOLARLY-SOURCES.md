## How to Use This Bibliography

This bibliography is organized by:
1. **Primary sources** - Original documents, texts, records
2. **Secondary sources** - Scholarly analysis and interpretation
3. **Reference works** - Encyclopedias, dictionaries, databases
4. **Dissertations and theses** - Emerging scholarship

Each entry includes relevance notes for tomato noodle research.

---

## I. Primary Sources

### A. Historical Chinese Texts

#### Botanical and Agricultural

**《群芳谱》(Qunfangpu, "Compendium of Flowers and Plants")**
- Author: 王象晋 (Wang Xiangjin)
- Date: 1621
- Relevance: First major Chinese botanical description of tomatoes
- Access: Chinese National Library; various reprints

**《本草纲目》(Bencao Gangmu, "Compendium of Materia Medica")**
- Author: 李时珍 (Li Shizhen)
- Date: 1596
- Relevance: Pharmacological context for nightshade family
- Access: Widely available; multiple translations

**《植物名实图考》(Zhiwu Mingshi Tukao)**
- Author: 吴其濬 (Wu Qijun)
- Date: 1848
- Relevance: 19th-century botanical descriptions
- Access: Chinese botanical archives

#### Literary Sources

**老舍散文集 (Lao She Essay Collections)**
- Contains: "西红柿" ("The Tomato," 1935)
- Relevance: PRIMARY SOURCE for cultural attitudes
- Access: 老舍全集 (Complete Works); various editions

**周作人散文集 (Zhou Zuoren Essay Collections)**
- Contains: Various food essays, 1920s-1940s
- Relevance: Cultural context for food acceptance
- Access: Modern reprints available

### B. Government Records

#### Qing Dynasty

**农工商部档案 (Ministry of Agriculture, Industry, and Commerce Archives)**
- Date: 1908
- Contains: First official tomato seed import record
- Location: First Historical Archives of China, Beijing
- Relevance: Documents official agricultural introduction

#### Republic of China

**农业年鉴 (Agricultural Yearbooks)**
- Dates: 1930s-1940s
- Contains: Cultivation statistics, regional data
- Location: Academia Sinica, Taiwan; various libraries
- Relevance: Early commercial cultivation data

#### People's Republic of China

**中国农业统计年鉴 (China Agricultural Statistical Yearbook)**
- Dates: 1949-present
- Publisher: Ministry of Agriculture
- Relevance: Production data, consumption trends
- Access: National Bureau of Statistics; online databases

### C. Periodicals and Newspapers

#### Republican Era

**《申报》(Shenbao, Shanghai News)**
- Dates: 1872-1949
- Contains: Advertisements, recipes, cultural commentary
- Relevance: Urban food culture documentation
- Access: Digital archives at various universities

**《良友画报》(The Young Companion)**
- Dates: 1926-1945
- Contains: Lifestyle content, recipes, food trends
- Relevance: Visual and textual food culture
- Access: Various university collections

#### Contemporary

**《中国烹饪》(Chinese Cuisine)**
- Dates: 1980-present
- Contains: Professional recipes, technique articles
- Relevance: Modern recipe standardization
- Access: Chinese periodical databases

## II. Secondary Sources

### A. Books in English

#### Food History

**Swislocki, Mark. *Culinary Nostalgia: Regional Food Culture and the Urban Experience in Shanghai.***
- Publisher: Stanford University Press, 2009
- ISBN: 978-0804760126
- Relevance: Shanghai food culture, Western influence
- Key chapters: Ch. 3-4 on foreign food adoption

**Anderson, E.N. *The Food of China.***
- Publisher: Yale University Press, 1988
- ISBN: 978-0300047394
- Relevance: Comprehensive Chinese food history
- Key chapters: Introduction of foreign crops

**Dunlop, Fuchsia. *Shark's Fin and Sichuan Pepper: A Sweet-Sour Memoir of Eating in China.***
- Publisher: W.W. Norton, 2008
- ISBN: 978-0393066579
- Relevance: Contemporary Chinese food culture
- Note: Contains observations on tomato-egg ubiquity

**Phillips, Carolyn. *All Under Heaven: Recipes from the 35 Cuisines of China.***
- Publisher: Ten Speed Press, 2016
- ISBN: 978-1607749820
- Relevance: Regional recipe variations
- Note: Scholarly introduction to regional cuisines

**Roberts, J.A.G. *China to Chinatown: Chinese Food in the West.***
- Publisher: Reaktion Books, 2002
- ISBN: 978-1861891334
- Relevance: Diaspora food culture, adaptation
- Key chapters: Chinese food globalization

#### Agricultural History

**Bray, Francesca. *Science and Civilisation in China, Vol. 6, Part 2: Agriculture.***
- Publisher: Cambridge University Press, 1984
- ISBN: 978-0521250764
- Relevance: Agricultural technology and history
- Note: Comprehensive but predates tomato focus

**Perkins, Dwight H. *Agricultural Development in China, 1368-1968.***
- Publisher: Aldine, 1969
- Relevance: Long-term agricultural patterns
- Note: Context for crop introduction

### B. Books in Chinese

**《中国蔬菜史》(History of Chinese Vegetables)**
- Author: Various agricultural historians
- Publisher: 中国农业出版社
- Relevance: Vegetable crop histories including tomato

**《中国饮食文化史》(History of Chinese Food Culture)**
- Editor: 赵荣光 (Zhao Rongguang)
- Publisher: Various editions
- Relevance: Comprehensive food culture history

**《上海饮食文化史》(History of Shanghai Food Culture)**
- Various authors
- Relevance: Treaty port food culture, Western influence

### C. Journal Articles

#### Food Studies

**Wu, David Y.H. "Chinese Food." *The Oxford Handbook of Food History*, ed. Jeffrey Pilcher.**
- Publisher: Oxford University Press, 2012
- Relevance: Overview of Chinese food historiography

**Cwiertka, Katarzyna J. "From Ethnic to Hip: Circuits of Japanese Cuisine in Europe."**
- Journal: *Food and Foodways* 13:4 (2005)
- Relevance: Comparative model for food globalization

**Farquhar, Judith. "Eating Chinese Medicine."**
- Journal: *Cultural Anthropology* 9:4 (1994)
- Relevance: Food-medicine connections in Chinese culture

#### Agricultural History

**Bray, Francesca. "Agriculture." *Cambridge History of China*.**
- Publisher: Cambridge University Press
- Relevance: Crop introduction patterns

**Various. *Agricultural History of China* (中国农史)**
- Journal published by Nanjing Agricultural University
- Relevance: Ongoing research on crop histories

### D. Conference Papers

**Association for Asian Studies Annual Conference**
- Various papers on food culture
- Relevance: Cutting-edge food studies research

**Oxford Symposium on Food and Cookery**
- Various years
- Papers on Chinese food occasionally presented

## VIII. How to Cite

### Chicago Style (Notes-Bibliography)

**Book:**
```
Swislocki, Mark. Culinary Nostalgia: Regional Food Culture and
  the Urban Experience in Shanghai. Stanford: Stanford University
  Press, 2009.
```

**Chinese text:**
```
王象晋. 《群芳谱》. 1621. [Wang Xiangjin. Qunfangpu. 1621.]
```

**Journal article:**
```
Wu, David Y.H. "Chinese Food." In The Oxford Handbook of Food
  History, edited by Jeffrey Pilcher, 543-68. Oxford: Oxford
  University Press, 2012.
```

### APA Style

**Book:**
```
Swislocki, M. (2009). Culinary nostalgia: Regional food culture
  and the urban experience in Shanghai. Stanford University Press.
```
